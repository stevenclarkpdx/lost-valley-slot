# Lost Valley Design Vision

Theme: dinosaur paleontology / lost expedition.

Fantasy:
The player is not collecting coins. They are mounting an expedition, finding evidence, and uncovering a hidden prehistoric valley.

Main game:
The 5x5 main board represents the expedition phase.
Symbols:
- Low: Compass, Brush, Journal, Pickaxe, Canteen
- Premium: Jeep, Helicopter, Scientist, Satellite Map, Supply Crate
- Scatter: Footprint

Trigger:
3+ Footprints trigger the Lost Valley feature.

Feature:
The screen shifts to a fog-covered valley grid. Each successful respin reveals fog tiles.

Discovery jackpots:
- Mini: New Fossil
- Minor: Complete Skeleton
- Major: Living Specimen
- Grand: New Species

Valley variants:
1. Fossil Valley
   - Reliable, steady value.
   - Medium hit rate.
   - Lower ceiling.
   - Feels safe and consistent.

2. Predator Valley
   - Low hit rate.
   - Huge upside.
   - Rare major discoveries.
   - Feels dangerous, tense, and swingy.

3. Nesting Grounds
   - High hit frequency.
   - Chain reactions.
   - More respin resets.
   - Feels active, chaotic, and generous, but often with smaller wins.

Visual target:
Gritty, cinematic, dark jungle / excavation / fossil UI. Commercial slot readability, but less bouncy and toy-like.
Avoid generic coins/treasure where possible.

Presentation principle:
The left destination-cue panel should be stable across spins and valley types. It should track all visible valley-trigger cues at once (currently Fossil Footprints and Predator Tracks) instead of swapping identity when one cue family appears. This keeps anticipation readable and gives future valleys a natural home in the same panel.
